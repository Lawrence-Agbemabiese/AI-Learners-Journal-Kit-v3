Sample Learning Session - Tailored to Your Learning Goals

User's Initial Prompt:
"Give me the command on Terminal to find every copy of Warp on my computer and show me the full paths."
A:
ls -ld /Applications/Warp.app ~/Applications/Warp.app 2>/dev/null
Q: What does 2>/dev/null mean?
A: It hides error messages.
  • 2 - refers to stderr (error output stream)
  • > - redirects output to a file or location
  • /dev/null - a special "black hole" that discards everything
Example: ls nonexistent_file 2>/dev/null (No error message shown if file doesn't exist)
Q: What about >/dev/null 2>&1 or &>/dev/null?
A: These hide everything - both output and errors.
Analogy: Like throwing away both the food and the complaints at a restaurant - nothing gets through to bother you. The kitchen could be on fire, but you won't hear about it!
