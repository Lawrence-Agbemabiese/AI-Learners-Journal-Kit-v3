## 1. Ranked customer segments (Student Portfolio Pack focus, Ghana)

### Segment 1 — One Million Coders Programme (OMCP) trainees — REQUIRED SEGMENT, strongest fit

**Who:** Participants in Ghana's flagship government coding program, launched 16 April 2025 by President Mahama with GH¢100M funding, training in coding, AI, cybersecurity, and web development ([Techlabari](https://techlabari.com/ghana-rolled-out-a-programme-to-train-a-million-coders-critics-say-its-already-hitting-road-blocks/); [Graphic Online launch report](https://www.graphic.com.gh/news/general-news/ghana-news-president-launches-one-million-coders-initiative-attracts-90-000-applications-in-48-hours.html); official portal: [onemillioncoders.gov.gh](https://onemillioncoders.gov.gh/) — portal is JS-rendered and shows minimal public content, so all figures below come from press coverage).

**Scale & timeline (date-stamped):**
- 90,000+ applications within 48 hours of registration opening (April 2025) ([Graphic Online](https://www.graphic.com.gh/news/general-news/ghana-news-president-launches-one-million-coders-initiative-attracts-90-000-applications-in-48-hours.html))
- Pilot started with 560 trainees ([Graphic Online](https://www.graphic.com.gh/news/general-news/one-million-coders-programme-begins-starts-560-trainees.html)); 859 completed the pilot by Aug 2025, 52% female ([Techlabari](https://techlabari.com/ghana-rolled-out-a-programme-to-train-a-million-coders-critics-say-its-already-hitting-road-blocks/))
- **Phase Two began 11 May 2026; 12,623 course completions recorded by 30 May 2026** ([Prime News Ghana, 31 May 2026](https://www.primenewsghana.com/general-news/more-than-12-000-learners-complete-courses-under-one-million-coders-programme.html); [Ghana Business News](https://www.ghanabusinessnews.com/2026/05/30/more-than-12000-learners-complete-courses-under-one-million-coders-programme/))

**Profile & tracks:** "students, teachers, entrepreneurs, market women, public servants, young professionals, parents, and persons with disabilities"; tracks: cybersecurity, data analytics, AI, cloud computing, software development, UI/UX — delivered via virtual instruction, self-paced online learning, and physical centers ([Prime News Ghana](https://www.primenewsghana.com/general-news/more-than-12-000-learners-complete-courses-under-one-million-coders-programme.html)). Private-sector partners named in coverage: Huawei, MTN, AWS, and Oracle ([Techlabari](https://techlabari.com/ghana-rolled-out-a-programme-to-train-a-million-coders-critics-say-its-already-hitting-road-blocks/)).

**How they document learning:** The government promises trainees "globally recognized certifications" ([GBC Ghana](https://www.gbcghanaonline.com/general/coders-ghana/2026/)), but no press coverage describes how trainees document work-in-progress, keep learning notes, or build portfolios ([Prime News Ghana](https://www.primenewsghana.com/general-news/more-than-12-000-learners-complete-courses-under-one-million-coders-programme.html) makes no mention of portfolios). **The gap is the *evidence of learning* — process documentation and portfolio — not the credential.** This sharpens positioning: the pack complements the promised certificate rather than competing with it. Fit remains inferred from absence, not observed behavior (see Unknowns).

**Fit:**
- *Student tier:* self-paced online learners across 6+ tracks with a promised certificate but no visible portfolio/process-documentation system; a GHS 49 12-week portfolio pack rides the program's cadence. Delivery-gap frustration is documented — one registrant: *"We were told we'd start classes soon after the launch. Everyone had been excited. Then—nothing."* ([Techlabari](https://techlabari.com/ghana-rolled-out-a-programme-to-train-a-million-coders-critics-say-its-already-hitting-road-blocks/)) — a self-directed journal is a hedge against program stop-start.

## 3. Top 5 pain points (customers' words where available, cited)

1. **Skills evaporate without a capture system.** "Have you ever felt that a command or a syntax has just vanished from your head? … When you don't use a piece of information often … You lose it." — Mangabo Kolawole (African software engineer/author), who prescribes exactly our mechanism: "Have a learning journal: you can write in it what you learned, how to apply it and write what you are going to learn next." ([DEV Community](https://dev.to/koladev/are-you-forgetting-how-to-code-and-programming-concepts-4chc))
2. **Forgetting is a systems problem, not a discipline problem.** Developer PKM content frames re-forgetting closures/concepts as needing an external system, not more studying ([Medium, Mar 2026](https://medium.com/@koteshavula/how-to-build-a-second-brain-for-programming-concepts-you-keep-forgetting-44a40fb10499); [DEV Community, Aug 2025](https://dev.to/rajguru_yadav_56d13a7b8fc/why-every-developer-should-build-a-second-brain-1jaf)). *Caveat: these are creator/practitioner voices, not Ghana-specific learner quotes — local verbatim quotes are Unknown #4.*
3. **Program discontinuity strands learners.** "We were told we'd start classes soon after the launch. Everyone had been excited. Then—nothing." — OMCP registrant, after the portal went offline by Nov 2025 ([Techlabari](https://techlabari.com/ghana-rolled-out-a-programme-to-train-a-million-coders-critics-say-its-already-hitting-road-blocks/)). A local-first journal keeps the learning record alive when platforms stall.
4. **Documentation of learning is expected and incentivized, but tooling isn't provided.** Nigeria's 3MTT institutionalizes reflection: fellows work in P2P groups of 4–10 and are incentivized to post weekly learning reflections (corroborating sources describe reflections as a condition for rewards such as a 10GB MTN data bundle — not a mandate) and to present at state-based Knowledge Showcases with rewards ([3MTT support KB — P2P](https://3mttsupport.tawk.help/article/getting-started-with-peer-to-peer-learning); [3MTT learning community FAQ](https://3mttsupport.tawk.help/article/frequently-asked-questions-for-the-3mtt-learning-community) — **note: both KB pages return HTTP 403 to automated fetchers; specifics like "2-minute videos" and laptop/modem prizes are unverified**). Institutional evidence (program rules, not learner quotes) that reflection/documentation is the expected behavior our product structures.
5. **Device and cost constraints shape what tools are viable.** OMCP's laptop procurement at GHS 14,000/unit vs ~GHS 10,000 market price became a national controversy; 20,000 laptops procured ([Techlabari](https://techlabari.com/ghana-rolled-out-a-programme-to-train-a-million-coders-critics-say-its-already-hitting-road-blocks/)); critics flag missing impact frameworks ([Citi Newsroom](https://www.citinewsroom.com/2025/04/critically-examining-ghanas-1-million-coders-programme-as-a-pathway-to-building-a-digitally-skilled-population/)). Signal: access hardware is scarce/contested, so a lightweight, offline-capable, no-subscription tool (v3.0.1 works offline; AI optional via free-tier key) matches the constraint. *Interpretation, flagged as such — not a learner quote.*

## 4. Falsifiable demand hypotheses (for Offer Architect)

- **H1 — OMCP price-point hypothesis.** Ghanaian AI/coding learners reachable via OMCP-adjacent and campus communities will buy a GHS 49 portfolio pack at ≥2% visitor→buyer. *Basis:* the verified ALX USD 5/month anchor (within ~10% of USD 4.50) plus confirmed cedi/MoMo digital-product rails on Selar; **the underlying assumption — that a one-time GHS 49 purchase behaves like a USD 5 subscription decision — is unvalidated**, which is exactly what this test measures. *Falsified if:* <1% after 1,000 landing visits (per `CLAUDE.md` threshold) — then price or framing (not channel) is reworked first.
- **H2 — Portfolio-gap hypothesis.** Positioning the pack as "the portfolio evidence OMCP's certificate doesn't give you" outperforms generic "journal your AI learning" copy. *Test:* two lead-magnet landing variants; portfolio-gap variant must hit ≥25% opt-in (per `CLAUDE.md`) and beat the generic variant on 500 visits. *Falsified if:* it converts at parity or worse.
- **H3 — Facilitator-first hypothesis.** Institutional trainers (OMCP centers, bootcamps, university clubs, workshop conveners like the UPH/TEA-LP model) convert faster than students — and per the revenue arithmetic in §2, one license is worth ~23 student sales at an assumed USD 100 price point. *Test:* direct outreach to 20 named Ghana facilitators (OMCP centers + Codetrain + IIPGH + GDG campus leads) in 60 days. *Success:* ≥3 discovery calls and ≥1 paid/licensed cohort. *Falsified if:* <2 replies — then the Facilitator Pack is deprioritized for this cycle despite the workshop signal.

## 5. Expansion-market shortlist (evidence only — no winner picked)

**Nigeria.** (1) **3MTT** (Federal Ministry of Communications + NITDA): Phase 1 = 30,000 fellows across all 36 states + FCT (Dec 2023); Phase 2 = 270,000 across cohorts of 60k/90k/120k — 300,000 total; 12 tracks including AI/ML, data science, software development; free to fellows; ~3-month courses, hybrid delivery, 120+ partner organizations ([3mtt.nitda.gov.ng](https://3mtt.nitda.gov.ng/)). Critically, 3MTT *already institutionalizes* the journaling behavior: fellows work in P2P groups of 4–10 and are incentivized — not mandated — to post weekly learning reflections and present at state-based Knowledge Showcases with rewards ([3MTT KB](https://3mttsupport.tawk.help/article/getting-started-with-peer-to-peer-learning); [FAQ](https://3mttsupport.tawk.help/article/frequently-asked-questions-for-the-3mtt-learning-community) — KB pages block automated fetchers; prize specifics unverified) — a 300k-person population being *nudged and rewarded* to reflect weekly is the single best behavioral match found in this research. (2) **First-party workshop evidence:** the March 2026 UPH workshop showed 25 non-coding energy professionals successfully "vibe-coding" real geospatial analyses with ChatGPT/Colab in 3 days and leaving with personal replication plans ([agenticppa.com](https://agenticppa.com/blog/what-happens-when-you-hand-25-nigerian-energy-planners-an-ai-co-pilot)). **Explicit caveat:** this validates institutional/professional training demand — i.e., the *Facilitator Pack* buyer (institutes, funded programs, workshop conveners) — and says little about individual students paying ~USD 4.50; the 3MTT signal covers the student side. Together they cover both tiers, which is why Nigeria leads the shortlist.

**Kenya.** Power Learn Project has trained 38,000+ young Africans since 2021 (Nairobi HQ), graduated 9,000 in Dec 2025 with UN Women ESARO/AGCCI, and targets 1M developers ([Vanguard, Dec 2025](https://www.vanguardngr.com/2025/12/power-learn-project-esaro-agcci-graduate-9000-young-software-developers/amp/); [TechMoran, Jun 2025](https://techmoran.com/2025/06/05/power-learn-project-celebrates-training-over-20000-youth-through-its-skills-to-jobs-model203131/)). Paid-market ceiling is proven high: Moringa School charges ~KES 174,000 (~USD 1,300) for a 25-week bootcamp and has trained 7,000+ students ([Business Radar, Jan 2025](https://www.businessradar.co.ke/blog/2025/01/14/moringa-school-fees-in-2025-everything-you-need-to-know/)), with ALX Nairobi active ([Mastercard Foundation](https://mastercardfdn.org/en/news/alx-kick-starts-inaugural-all-tech-training-programs-for-over-32000-learners-across-africa/)). Strong M-Pesa digital-payment culture. Gap: no Kenya-specific journaling/portfolio pain quotes gathered yet.

**India.** Enormous scale and proven sub-USD-6 price tolerance: Udemy's recurring India flash sales at INR 399–499 (~USD 4.7–5.9) are described as "the cheapest way to learn a tool," with sales every ~2 weeks ([trydiscountcoupons.com](https://trydiscountcoupons.com/udemy-coupon-india-courses-at-inr-430-450-500-640/); [courseguidance.in](https://courseguidance.in/is-udemy-worth-it-india/)); NPTEL offers 3,200+ free courses with 5.62M YouTube subscribers ([nptel.ac.in](https://www.nptel.ac.in/)). The USD 4.50 price is pre-validated, but the market is crowded, anchored to deep discounting, and has no first-party relationship advantage — highest volume, weakest differentiation of the three.
