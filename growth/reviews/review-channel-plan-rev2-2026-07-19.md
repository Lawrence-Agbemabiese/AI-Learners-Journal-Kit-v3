That is not what §3 says as it stands on disk, and it violates the Checkpoint B posture (GHS 950 is the public opener; GHS 1,500 is an internal bound, never an opening quote). This plan's design conforms; the fix is assigned to conversion-system-builder in `review-funnel-rev2-2026-07-19.md`. **No action required from distribution-strategist on the design itself** — but see Refutation 3.
2. **Arithmetic slack in the §1 chain (non-blocking, log for the retro).** (a) The WhatsApp sub-chain's strict low product is 400 × 5% × 8 = 160 visits, not the stated "~300"; TikTok's is 7,200 × 0.5% = 36, not "~50" — the stated floors quietly assume better-than-worst-case inputs. (b) The headline "~1,000–2,500" ceiling exceeds the chain's own maximum of 2,250. (c) "Enough for Experiment 3 (2×500 visits) in all cases" is false at the plan's own ~500 low case, where neither experiment completes on time; the §6 fallback covers this in substance (extend-rather-than-read-early generalizes to H2) but names only H1. None of this changes a decision — the Day-45 re-forecast replaces all of these numbers with observed UTM data — but the low bounds should not present as conservative when they are mid-case.
3. **New interaction created jointly by the two revisions (advisory — put to Lawrence at the Day-0 check-in alongside the already-flagged §7 amendment):** the redesigned Experiment 2 makes the pilot concession the treatment variable, but the funnel's public surfaces advertise it ("First cohort with us? Ask about the pilot offer: 50% off… GHS 475" on landing-page-pricing-v2; "ask about the 50%-off pilot" in Touch 6). An Arm A (no-concession) prospect who visits the site can simply ask, diluting the treatment contrast — a far milder defect than the old 950/1,500 collision (nobody is over-quoted; both numbers are public), and the one-pager's guardrails already require logging whether the prospect had seen the public pricing page. Options for Lawrence: hold the public pilot line off the site until Experiment 2 closes, or accept that the experiment reads "proactively offered vs. available on request" and keep the exposure covariate logged. Either is workable; the plan should record whichever he picks.
4. **Hair-split on §0's parenthetical (wording only):** "until both surfaces show the approved pricing (per-cohort opener GHS 950, floor GHS 450, ceiling GHS 1,500; institution annual GHS 4,500)" can be misread as instructing that the floor and ceiling appear on the public surfaces — the opposite of the plan's own §3 and the funnel's floor/ceiling-stay-internal decision. This is an internal planning document (bounds may appear here, per my rev. 1 ruling), but the sentence is an instruction to a transcriber-adjacent audience and should not be ambiguous. Weakest sentence 1.
