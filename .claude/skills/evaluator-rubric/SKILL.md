# Evaluator Rubric — Growth Artifacts

Grade each criterion 1–5. An artifact passes only if EVERY criterion scores ≥4.

## 1. Pain urgency
Is the customer problem urgent and evidenced by cited sources? 5 = multiple recent, local, primary sources in customers' own words. 1 = asserted without evidence.

## 2. Promise believability
Would a skeptical Ghanaian student or facilitator believe the claim? Is it specific and falsifiable? 5 = concrete outcome, honest scope, matches v3.0.1 features exactly. 1 = hype ("get rich", "master AI"), or promises features that don't exist.

## 3. Differentiation
Is it distinct from free alternatives the buyer already has (ChatGPT + notebook, Notion, plain markdown files)? 5 = names the alternative and beats it on a stated dimension. 1 = ignores free alternatives.

## 4. Economics
Does price × realistic conversion × reachable audience produce meaningful revenue? Show the arithmetic. 5 = numbers present, sourced, conservative. 1 = no arithmetic.

## 5. Global fit
Does copy avoid US-only idioms? Does pricing respect purchasing power (GHS anchor, PPP for expansion markets)? Payment methods realistic for the market (mobile money matters in Ghana)? 5 = all three. 1 = uniform USD pricing, US idioms.

## 6. Product truth
Every feature claim checked against README.md and docs/Paid_Product_Checklist.md. Python 3.9+ and bring-your-own-API-key stated where relevant. No AI-accuracy guarantees. 5 = fully compliant. Any violation = automatic 1 (fail).

## 7. Slop check
Would Lawrence publish this under his name without edits? Generic filler, unearned superlatives, or interchangeable-with-any-product copy = fail. 5 = specific voice, concrete details, nothing wasted.

## Scoring notes

- Style never compensates for missing evidence.
- When in doubt between two scores, assign the lower one.
- Record scores in growth/reviews/ using the evaluator agent's output contract.
